TRAKI
Product Specification

The one-tap time tracker for language learners.

Traki measures the time learners invest across the four activities that actually build fluency — and it is designed so that opening the app and starting a session take a single tap.

PlatformiOS (iPhone)
AudienceIndependent language learners
PreparedJuly 2026
StatusConcept / design review
In one paragraph

Learners open the app, tap one of four study modes, and a stopwatch begins immediately. When they finish, they stop and see the session added to their day. A statistics dashboard turns those sessions into streaks, trends and a consistency heatmap, while widgets let them start and monitor a session without ever opening the app. Time they forgot to track can be added or corrected by hand. The product's north star is simple: make tracking so frictionless that learners actually do it every day.

01

Guiding principles

Speed to start
One tap from opening the app to a running timer. Every extra step is treated as a bug.
One activity at a time
A single stopwatch runs at once, so the learner is never unsure what is being counted.
Honest numbers
Totals reflect real study time, and manual logging exists to fill in what the timer missed.
Progress at a glance
Streaks, daily goal and trends are visible without digging through menus.
Track without opening
Widgets can start a session and show a live timer from the Lock and Home Screens.
Effortless correction
Any entry can be edited or removed later, so a mistake never discourages logging.
02

The four learning modes

Every tracked session belongs to exactly one mode. Each mode keeps a consistent color throughout the app, so learners recognise it instantly in cards, charts and history.

FlashcardsReviewing vocabulary and grammar decks — typically spaced-repetition study.
ListeningPodcasts, shows, music and audio immersion in the target language.
ReadingBooks, articles, subtitles and any text-based study.
Sentence MiningHarvesting new words and example sentences from native material.
03

Screens

3.1 · Home

Purpose — the launchpad. It answers one question first: “what do you want to study right now?” and gets out of the way.

What's on it
  • A greeting, the current day-streak, and a slim daily-goal progress bar with today's total.
  • A prominent Resume card that repeats the learner's most recent mode in one tap.
  • Four mode cards — Flashcards, Listening, Reading, Sentence Mining — each showing today's time in that mode.
  • A Log a past session button for adding time after the fact.
Interactions
  • Tapping any mode card starts the stopwatch immediately and moves to the Active Timer.
  • Tapping Resume starts the last mode used.

3.2 · Active Timer

Purpose — a calm, full-screen view for a session in progress, tinted with the mode's color.

What's on it
  • A large running clock and the current mode, with a live “tracking” indicator.
  • A running projection of what today's total for that mode will reach.
Interactions
  • Pause / Resume to handle interruptions without losing the session.
  • Stop & save ends the session and opens the summary.

3.3 · Session Complete

Purpose — a short, rewarding confirmation that the time was captured.

What's on it
  • The session length, the mode, and three quick stats: that mode's total today, the day's total, and the current streak.
Interactions
  • Go again immediately starts another session in the same mode.
  • Done returns to Home.

3.4 · Statistics

Purpose — help learners understand their habits and see progress build over time.

What's on it
  • A Day / Month / Year switch, with the total for the chosen period and how it compares to the previous one.
  • A last-7-days recap showing each day's time, split by mode.
  • A breakdown by mode, so learners see where their time really goes.
  • A trend line of daily study hours over recent weeks.
  • A consistency heatmap — one square per day — that rewards showing up.
Interactions
  • Switching the period instantly updates the total, comparison and breakdown.

3.5 · History

Purpose — the complete, trustworthy record of every session, grouped by day.

What's on it
  • Sessions listed newest-first under day headings, each showing mode, time of day and duration, with the day's total.
Interactions
  • Press and hold any entry to open its editor — change the mode or duration, or delete it. Totals everywhere update to match.

3.6 · Settings

Purpose — the small set of choices that personalise tracking.

  • Learning — target language, daily goal, and which modes are active.
  • Appearance — theme: Light, Dark, or System.
  • Tracking — auto-pause when idle, show a Live Activity on the Lock Screen, and round sessions to the nearest minute.
04

Logging & correcting time

Log a past session

Learners often study away from their phone. From Home, “Log a past session” opens a sheet where they choose a mode, pick Today or Yesterday, and set a duration with simple −/+ steppers or quick-pick amounts. Saving adds the time to that day's totals and drops it into History.

Edit or delete an entry

Pressing and holding an entry in History reopens the same sheet for that session, letting learners fix the mode or duration — or delete it outright. Because every total is derived from these entries, a single correction keeps the whole app accurate.

05

Widgets

Widgets carry the “track without opening the app” principle onto the Lock and Home Screens.

Live Activity · Lock ScreenShows a session already running, with its live timer, so learners can glance at progress and stop it without unlocking into the app.
Lock Screen · circularA compact today-total ring plus quick-start buttons for jumping straight into a mode.
Home Screen · quick startA medium widget with all four modes as buttons — one tap begins a session.
Home Screen · this weekA medium widget showing the weekly heatmap and total, for a motivating glance.
Home Screen · todayA small widget with today's total, streak and goal progress.
06

Appearance

Appearance is set in Settings with a single choice of Light, Dark, or System — the last option follows the phone's own light/dark setting automatically. The default is System. Every screen and widget adapts, and the four mode colors stay recognisable in both themes.

07

How it fits together

A bottom tab bar anchors the four everyday destinations. Tracking flows sit on top of them as focused, full-screen moments.

  • Tab bar: Home · Statistics · History · Settings — always one tap away.
  • Tracking flow: Home → tap a mode → Active Timer → Stop → Session Complete → back to Home.
  • Sheets: logging and editing slide up over the current screen and dismiss when done.
  • Widgets: can launch a session or deep-link into the app from outside it.
THE CORE LOOP
Open→ Tap a mode→ Timer runs→ Stop & save→ See progress
08

Future considerations

Ideas that extend the product without compromising its focus on effortless tracking:

  • An exact start-time picker when logging past sessions.
  • Per-mode daily goals, not just a single overall goal.
  • An Apple Watch complication to start and stop from the wrist.
  • A Focus-style auto-start that begins tracking when a learner opens their flashcard or reading app.
  • Support for multiple target languages, each with its own statistics.
